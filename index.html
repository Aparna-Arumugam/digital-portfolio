Tribute to Mother Teresa

Biography

Mother Teresa, known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, Macedonia. She dedicated her life to serving the poorest of the poor in the slums of Calcutta, India.

Her selfless and compassionate work led to the establishment of the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic religious congregation, which provides care for those suffering from poverty, sickness, and various forms of distress.

Awards and Achievements

Mother Teresa received numerous awards and honors for her humanitarian work, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. She was canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church in 2016.

Legacy

Mother Teresa's legacy lives on, inspiring people worldwide to dedicate their lives to serving others. Her tireless efforts to alleviate the suffering of the less fortunate and her unwavering commitment to love and compassion continue to be a source of inspiration for generations.
